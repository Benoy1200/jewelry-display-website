Standard Jewelry Showcase Dimensions & Size Guide

Choosing the right dimensions for your jewelry showcases is crucial for both customer comfort and store flow. This guide breaks down the industry standards for height, width, and depth.

1. Standard Showcase Heights

The height of the showcase is determined by its function. Most retail jewelry cases fall into three categories:

  • Sit-down cases (29" - 32"): These allow for an intimate sales process where both the salesperson and customer are seated.
  • Standing countertops (38" - 42"): The most common height for high-volume stores where customers browse while standing.
  • Tower displays (70" - 80"): Used for featured pieces, often placed against walls or in center-store "islands."

2. Standard Widths and Depths

Showcase Type Standard Width Standard Depth
Countertop 48", 60", 72" 20" - 24"
Tower Cabinet 15" - 24" 15" - 20"
Wall Case 48" - 96" 12" - 18"

3. The "Golden Height" for Visibility

The "eye-level" rule suggests that featured items should be placed between 42" and 54" from the floor for maximum visibility while standing. Anything lower requires the customer to lean in, which can be used to create intimacy for expensive diamond viewings.

Conclusion

While standard sizes are cost-effective, remember that custom heights can be tailored to your specific brand's aesthetic. Always leave at least 36" of clearance behind the counter for staff movement.
