Glass Types for Jewelry Display

Last Updated: December 2025 12 min read By JewelryDisplayPro Team

Choosing the right glass for a jewelry showcase is crucial for visual impact, security, and cost efficiency. This comprehensive guide compares the most common glass options, their optical characteristics, durability, and ideal applications.

What You'll Learn

  • Low-iron glass for maximum clarity and true color rendering
  • Laminated glass for security applications
  • Tempered safety glass specifications and standards
  • UV filtering options for artifact and gemstone protection
  • Coated glass for anti-reflection and special effects

1. Low-Iron Glass (Ultra-Clear Glass)

Low-iron glass is the premium choice for high-end jewelry display:

  • Optical Properties: Extremely low iron content creates near-colorless transparency, maximizing light transmission (>92%).
  • Advantages: Enhances the brilliance and true color of gemstones, ideal for luxury jewelry presentation.
  • Considerations: Slightly higher cost than standard glass; more susceptible to scratches.
  • Best For: Flagship stores, boutique displays, and showcases requiring exceptional optical clarity.

2. Laminated Glass

Laminated glass provides both security and safety benefits:

  • Construction: Two layers of glass bonded with a PVB (polyvinyl butyral) or EVA interlayer.
  • Safety Features: Holds together when shattered, preventing sharp fragments and quick theft.
  • Optical Properties: Slight light refraction; transparency slightly lower than low-iron glass.
  • Best For: High-value jewelry displays requiring anti-theft protection.

3. Tempered Safety Glass

Tempered glass is heat-treated for enhanced strength:

  • Manufacturing Process: Heat treatment creates surface compression stress; breaks into small granules.
  • Safety Standards: Complies with international safety glass standards (ASTM C1048).
  • Optical Properties: Approximately 90% light transmission.
  • Best For: Budget-conscious displays balancing cost and safety requirements.

4. UV Filtering Glass

UV filtering glass protects light-sensitive jewelry and gemstones:

  • Technology: UV blocking agents added to glass or interlayer filter 95%+ of ultraviolet radiation.
  • Benefits: Prevents color fading and material aging from prolonged UV exposure.
  • Considerations: Higher cost; may slightly affect light color temperature.
  • Best For: Long-term display of colored gemstones, pearls, coral, and other UV-sensitive materials.

5. Coated Glass

Specialized coatings enhance performance for specific applications:

  • Anti-Reflective (AR) Coating: Reduces surface reflection to 1%–2%, improving visibility.
  • Mirror Coating: Creates reflective surfaces for backlit or lightbox displays.
  • Hydrophobic Coating: Repels fingerprints and moisture for easier maintenance.
  • Best For: Premium displays requiring maximum visibility or special lighting effects.

Glass Types Comparison

Glass Type Light Transmission Security Level Cost Best Application
Low-Iron ≈92% Standard Medium Luxury jewelry, flagship stores
Laminated ≈90% High (Anti-theft) Medium-High High-value displays, security-focused
Tempered ≈90% High Low Budget-conscious retail
UV Filtering ≈88% Medium Medium-High Colored gemstones, pearls
AR Coated ≈97% Medium High Premium displays, museums
